## Recommended Feature Enhancements

Beyond the technical code, here are the "Clinical Gold Standard" features you should aim for:

### 1. The "Blue Bottom" Depth

In traditional sandplay, the bottom of the tray is painted blue. When a client "digs" in the sand, they reveal water.

* **Feature Request:** Add a "Dig" tool. Clicking and dragging on the sand reveals a blue layer underneath. This is a massive symbolic feature for therapists.

### 2. Item Categorization (The "Search" Power)

As your library grows, finding a "Mother figure" among 200 items is hard.

* **Feature Request:** Implement a robust tagging system. A figurine of a "Lion" should show up under "Animal," "Aggressive," "Power," and "King."

### 3. The "Clinician's View" vs. "Client's View"

If this is used in telehealth:

* **Feature Request:** A "Sync" toggle. When ON, the client sees exactly what the therapist sees. When OFF, the therapist can open their "Clinical Insights" or take notes without the client seeing the UI overlay.

### 4. Grouping & Scene Saving

* **Feature Request:** Allow the clinician to save a "World" and reload it in the next session. This allows for long-term therapeutic work where a scene evolves over months.
