### **Gap Analysis & Critical Refinements**

#### **1. The "PHI Shadow" (Safety & Security)**

Even though you are "Zero-PHI," therapists are creatures of habit. They *will* accidentally type a client's full name into a session title (e.g., "John Doe - Sandtray Session").

* **The Fix:** Add a **Client-Side Sanitizer**. Before the session name is saved to the DB, have a small script check for common name patterns or simply add a persistent warning label under the input: *"Reminder: Do not use client names. Use initials or IDs only."*
* **The Instruction for Claude:** "Review the `New Session Dialog`. Implement a regex-based warning if it detects two capitalized words (Potential Name) and add a clear HIPAA-compliance reminder text."

#### **2. The "Snapshot" Utility Gap**

Right now, `html2canvas` is a great tool, but a raw PNG is hard for a therapist to manage.

* **The Fix:** The exported file needs **Metadata Watermarking**. When a snapshot is generated, the bottom 20px of the image should include: `ClinicalPlay.app | Session ID: [ID] | Date: [Timestamp]`.
* **Why?** If a therapist has 10 PNGs on their desktop at the end of the day, they need to know which one belongs to which session without opening them all.

#### **3. The "Participant Ghosting" Issue**

In WebSocket environments, "Ghosting" happens when a client closes their laptop lid. The clinician might still see them as "Active," leading to awkward "Are you still there?" moments.

* **The Fix:** Implement a **Heartbeat Monitor**. If a participant’s socket disconnects for more than 10 seconds, change their avatar status in the header to "Offline" or "Disconnected."
* **The Instruction for Claude:** "Audit the WebSocket `join` and `leave` logic. Ensure the Participant List in the Playroom header updates in real-time if a socket heartbeat is lost."

#### **4. The "Clinical Insights" Contextual Logic**

This is your most valuable feature for the $7/mo tier. It needs to be **Dynamic**, not static.

* **The Gap:** If the clinician is in the **Feeling Wheel**, the insights shouldn't just be "Ask about feelings." They should change based on the *intensity* of the emotion selected (if possible).
* **The Goal:** If the client picks "Grief," the panel should suggest: *"Grief is a heavy stone; ask where they feel that weight in their body."*
